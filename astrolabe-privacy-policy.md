# Privacy Policy for Astrolabe

*Last updated: June 15, 2026*

Astrolabe is a music player for Android. It runs entirely on your device. This policy describes what data the app handles, what it does not do, and what to do if you have questions.

## The short version

**Astrolabe does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data.** Everything the app does happens on your device. Nothing is sent to a server, an analytics service, an advertiser, or anyone else. The app does not require an account, does not require an internet connection to function, and does not track you.

If you'd rather read a longer version, the rest of this page covers the details.

## What Astrolabe reads on your device

When you grant Astrolabe permission to access audio files, it reads:

- **Audio files in folders you specify.** The app needs access to your music files in order to play them. You choose which folders Astrolabe has access to; you can change this at any time in your device's settings.
- **Standard audio file metadata** (artist, album, title, duration, etc.) — read from the files themselves, the same way any music player reads them.
- **Audio data from your files**, which the app analyzes locally to organize your library into a navigable map ("the constellation"). This analysis happens entirely on your device.

The app does not read other files on your device, your contacts, your messages, your location, your camera, your microphone, your call history, or any data outside the folders you've authorized.

## What Astrolabe stores on your device

For the app to work, it keeps some information in its local storage on your device:

- **Library data** — the list of music files it has access to, their metadata, and the analysis results used to build the constellation
- **Your playback history and play counts** — used to show which songs you've listened to and to surface them in the app's "lived-in" features
- **Your playlists** — any playlists you've created in the app
- **Your settings and preferences** within the app

All of this data lives only on your device, in the app's private storage area. None of it is transmitted anywhere. If you uninstall the app, this data is removed from your device automatically.

## What Astrolabe does NOT do

To be explicit, the app does not:

- Send any data to a server, ever
- Use any analytics or telemetry services
- Show ads or share data with advertisers
- Track you across apps or websites
- Require an account or login
- Require an internet connection (the app works fully offline)
- Use your microphone or camera
- Read your contacts, messages, location, or any data unrelated to your music files
- Sell, rent, share, or disclose your data to third parties (it has none of your data to share)

## Permissions the app requests, and why

Android requires apps to explicitly request access to certain things. Astrolabe asks for:

- **Read access to audio files** (`READ_MEDIA_AUDIO`) — required to play your music. Without this, the app has nothing to play.
- **Foreground service** (`FOREGROUND_SERVICE`, `FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MEDIA_PLAYBACK`) — required so music keeps playing when the app is in the background or your screen is off. Standard for any music player.
- **Post notifications** (`POST_NOTIFICATIONS`) — required to show the now-playing controls in your notification shade while music is playing.

The app does not request internet access, location, contacts, microphone, camera, or any other permission.

## Children's privacy

Astrolabe is suitable for general audiences. Because the app does not collect any personal information at all, it does not knowingly collect information from children under 13 — or from anyone of any age. If a parent or guardian is concerned about a child using the app, the answer is simple: the app reads only the music files you give it access to, and stores nothing outside the device.
